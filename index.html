About Me

Shengjun Kris Dong is a Ph.D. graduate student researcher at UC Berkeley, advised by Prof. Sophia Yakun Shao, and Prof. Christopher W. Fletcher. Kris' research interest lies in hardware-software co-design and domain-specific acceleration.

Currently Kris focuses her research on accelerating autonomous robotic and machine-learning applications. Her work encompasses both methodologies and infrastructure, spanning from algorithm optimization to hardware design. She built RoSÉ, a unified simulation platform that combines hardware and software, enabling pre-silicon, full-stack, closed-loop evaluation of robotic systems.

Prior to joining Berkeley, she worked as a Software Engineer at Tesla and a Product Manager at Amazon. She has 6+ years of programming experience through internships and projects involving hardware architecture design, large-scale system development, and full-stack software development.

Before her PhD, Kris received a dual Master's degree in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science Department of UC Berkeley; She also holds a Bachelor of Science in Mathematics, a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science, and a Bachelor of Business Administration in Finance from UMass Amherst.
